About

I am a PhD student studying fluid mechanics, urban aerodynamics, and data-driven methods for reconstructing complex flow fields from sparse measurements. My work combines experiments, large-eddy simulation, and machine learning to better understand the interaction between the atmospheric boundary layer and street-canyon flow.

This website can be used to share research updates, publications, conference presentations, datasets, code repositories, and contact information.

Research Interests

Urban Flow

Turbulent flow within and above urban street canyons, including ventilation, scalar transport, and the role of upstream morphology.

Boundary Layer Dynamics

Coupling between the atmospheric boundary layer and local urban flow, with attention to coherent structures and large-scale organisation.

Data-Driven Modelling

Sensor-informed reduced-order modelling, neural networks, and flow reconstruction from sparse measurements.

Selected Projects

Sensor-Informed Flow Reconstruction

Developing reduced-order and neural-network-based methods to reconstruct large-scale atmospheric boundary layer flow structures from limited experimental measurements.

Large-Eddy Simulation of Urban Canyons

Investigating ventilation, thermal effects, and particle transport in idealised and realistic urban canyons using OpenFOAM.

Flow-Structure Interaction of Large Scales

Analysing how outer-layer structures modulate canyon flow and affect near-street turbulence and transport.
