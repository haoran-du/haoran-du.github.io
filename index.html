About

My work combines wind-tunnel experiments, numerical simulations, and reduced-order modelling to investigate how complex structures in atmospheric boundary-layer flow influence turbulence, pollutant dispersion, and thermal transport. My current research focuses on sensor-informed reconstruction of urban canopy flow and wind farm wakes.

Research

Wind tunnel experiments

The wind-tunnel experiments are conducted in collaboration with LHEEA, École Centrale de Nantes. The models are typically built at 1:200 scale, and the generated boundary layer shows good agreement with ESDU guidelines. Particle Image Velocimetry (PIV) and Hot-Wire Anemometry (HWA) techniques are used.

Numerical simulations

OpenFOAM-based Large-Eddy Simulations (LES), primarily using pimpleFoam and its buoyant variant buoyantPimpleFoam, are used to analyse turbulent flow, convective heat transfer, and passive scalar transport. Customised discrete phase model (DPM) solvers are also used to study particulate dispersion.

Reduced-order modelling

Spectral Proper Orthogonal Decomposition (SPOD), Linear Stochastic Estimation (LSE), Autoencoders (AE), Convolutional Neural Networks (CNN), and other sensor-informed models are applied for flow reconstruction and prediction.
