About

I am a doctoral student at Western University. My research combines fluid mechanics, wind engineering, large-eddy simulation, wind-tunnel experiments, and data-driven modelling to study atmospheric boundary layer flow and urban canopy flow. My current work includes idealised and realistic street-canyon configurations, particulate pollutant dispersion, convective heat transfer, and reconstruction of flow fields from sparse measurements.

I also have training in data science and machine learning through an online Master of Data Science in Machine Learning at the University of Pittsburgh. My academic background spans mechanical engineering, computational modelling, experimentation, and applied machine learning. fileciteturn0file0

Research Interests

Urban Flow and Wind Engineering

Street-canyon aerodynamics, upstream morphology effects, ventilation, turbulent transport, and coupling between the urban canopy and the overlying boundary layer.

Large-Eddy Simulation and Experiments

OpenFOAM-based LES, wind-tunnel measurements, particle image velocimetry, and hot-wire anemometry for atmospheric and urban flow problems.

Reduced-Order and Data-Driven Modelling

Proper orthogonal decomposition, linear stochastic estimation, autoencoders, convolutional neural networks, and sensor-informed reconstruction of flow structures from limited measurements.
